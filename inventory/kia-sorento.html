The Sorento is again available with eight trims for 2023: LX, S, X-Line S AWD, EX, X-Line EX AWD, SX, SX Prestige, and X-Line SX Prestige AWD. We like the X-Line S AWD trim best, as it comes with the naturally aspirated powertrain and less troublesome eight-speed automatic. Is the 2023 Kia Sorento a Good SUV? Yes, the 2023 Sorento is a pretty good midsize SUV. The Sorento sports an upscale interior with a host of standard tech and safety features. It can seat up to seven occupants, and it provides a bunch of cargo space, at least when the rear seats are folded. The worst issue that plagues these Kia Sorento models is engine failure. This occurs when most consumers have accumulated an average of 62,000 miles. We recommend staying clear from them, as they may cost you more money in repairs. The standard engine gets fuel efficiency of 24 city MPG, 29 highway miles per gallon, and 26mpg combined. The upgraded available engine choice gets 22 city miles per gallon, 29mpg highway, and 25mpg combined. A KIA Sorento will cost about $7,953 for maintenance and repairs during its first 10 years of service. This beats the industry average for popular SUV models by $1,180. There is also a 23.71% chance that a Sorento will require a major repair during that time. The SUV earned a rating of Good in every crashworthiness test along with superior ratings for both the optional and standard front crash prevention systems. Here's the 2022 Kia Sorento safety ratings from the IIHS below: Crashworthiness: Small overlap front: driver-side – Good. Kia models are some of the most fuel-efficient vehicles on the road today! But the ones that attract the most complaints are visibility (specifically windshields cracking spontaneously), faulty brakes, engine problems, and electrical issues. There have also been complaints about the powertrain, with owners citing transmission problems.
